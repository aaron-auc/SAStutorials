Hint

The forward scatting is proportional to the contrast: $$I(0)\propto\Sigma_i(\Delta\mathrm{SLD}_i V_i)^2$$ where $\Delta\mathrm{SLD}_i = \mathrm{SLD}_i - \mathrm{SLD}_\mathrm{solvent}$ is the contrast (or excess scattering length density) of subunit $i$, and $V_i$ is the volume of that subuni. A core-shell particle consists of two subunits, core and the shell.

In this problem, $\Delta\mathrm{SLD}_\mathrm{core} = -\Delta\mathrm{SLD}_\mathrm{shell}$, so the volumes of the core and shell must be equal to get $I(0)=0$.
The volume of the core is $V_\mathrm{core} = \frac{4\pi}{3}R_\mathrm{core}^3$, and the shell volume is $V_\mathrm{shell} = \frac{4\pi}{3}R_\mathrm{outer}^3- V_\mathrm{core}$.
