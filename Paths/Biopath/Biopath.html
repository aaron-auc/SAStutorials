This pathway is designed to provide the essential knowledge for conducting SAXS experiments on biological samples. It explains how a sample's shape and size influence the scattering pattern, how to process collected data and perform background subtraction, and, finally, how to interpret the data to gain insights into the sample.

The tutorials below are intended to be followed in the order they are presented. After completion, feel free to explore the Home-page for more advanced topics.
